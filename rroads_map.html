Data and Methods

The Roman road network data came from The Digital Atlas of Roman and Medieval Civilizations, which is the digital version of the Roman roads identified in the Barrington Atlas. By splitting the roads by their vertices and converting to point types, the number of vertices in each grid was calculated. As expected, the highest number of vertices is around Rome. Interestingly, there are also five areas that show high values, with one being Judea.
